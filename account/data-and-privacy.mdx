This page explains how FLORA handles your data, who owns the content you create, and what privacy practices govern the platform. Whether you're on the Free plan or an Enterprise contract, FLORA's core commitments to data ownership and IP protection are the same across every tier.

## Data and IP Ownership

### Your Inputs Are Yours

You retain full ownership and confidentiality over everything you bring into FLORA. No uploaded files, prompts, or related metadata are ever used to train or fine-tune models — by FLORA or by any of our infrastructure and model partners. Every model provider FLORA works with operates under a strict **no-training agreement**.

### Your Outputs Are Yours

You own the rights to every generated output created in your account. There are no usage restrictions or commercial limitations tied to your plan tier. FLORA and its partners make no claim to your generated outputs.

<Note>
  Data ownership and IP rights are **identical across all plans** — Free, Starter, Pro, Max, and Enterprise. Plan tiers differ in capacity, collaboration features, and support level, not in who owns the work.
</Note>

### Upstream Vendor Agreements

FLORA only integrates with vendors that support enterprise-grade data isolation and IP protection. If an upstream provider updates its terms, FLORA reviews the change and enforces alignment with its data-protection policy before adopting the update.

## Plan Differences

<Info>
  The only differences between plan tiers are capacity, collaboration, and support. There are **no differences in IP ownership or data-use policy** between Free, Starter, Pro, Max, and Enterprise.
</Info>

Enterprise plans add formal compliance documentation — DPA and SOC-2 — for teams that require it. They do not grant any additional data ownership rights, because those rights already belong to you on every plan.

## Privacy Practices

### What FLORA Collects

FLORA collects only what it needs to deliver and improve the service:

<Accordion title="Personal and Account Information">
  Name and email address, collected when you create an account or contact support.
</Accordion>
<Accordion title="Device and Browser Information">
  Browser type, operating system, and device identifiers — used for security, troubleshooting, and compatibility.
</Accordion>
<Accordion title="Usage Data">
  Information about how you use FLORA, including features accessed, generation counts, and session activity — used for analytics and product improvement.
</Accordion>
<Accordion title="Uploaded Content">
  Files, images, and other assets you upload to FLORA to use in your projects. This content is yours and is not used for training.
</Accordion>

### How FLORA Uses Your Data

FLORA uses collected data for three purposes:

- **Service delivery** — running the platform, processing generations, and providing support
- **Analytics** — understanding how the product is used so FLORA can improve it
- **Security** — detecting and preventing unauthorized access or abuse

**FLORA does not sell your data.**

### Cookies

FLORA uses three categories of cookies:

| Cookie Type | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **Essential** | Required for the platform to function — authentication, session management, and security. |
| **Functional** | Remember your preferences and settings between sessions. |
| **Performance / Analytical** | Measure how the platform is used to inform improvements. No personal data is shared with third parties for advertising. |

### COPPA Compliance

FLORA does not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child under 13 has created an account, contact [support@florafauna.ai](mailto:support@florafauna.ai) and FLORA will remove the account and associated data.
