A workspace is the core organizational unit in FLORA — it holds all your projects, team members, usage budgets, and settings. Everything you create in FLORA lives inside a workspace, so knowing how to navigate and configure yours will save you time and keep your team running smoothly.

## Switching Workspaces

Use the **workspace selector** in the top-left corner of the dashboard to move between workspaces. The selector shows your current workspace's logo, name, and member count. Click it to open a dropdown listing every workspace you belong to, along with each workspace's plan type and member count. Your active workspace has a checkmark next to it.

<Tip>
  If you belong to many workspaces, use the search bar at the top of the dropdown to quickly find the one you need.
</Tip>

If you've been invited to a workspace you haven't joined yet, it appears in the list with a **Join** button. FLORA remembers the last workspace you used, so you'll land there automatically on your next login.

## Workspace Roles

Every member of a workspace is assigned one of three roles. Admins assign roles when inviting someone, and can change them at any time from the **People & Seats** page.

| Role | Label | Permissions |
|---|---|---|
| Admin | Admin | Full permissions: manage billing, settings, and all workspace features. Can invite Admins, Editors, and Guests. |
| Editor | Can Edit | Create and edit projects. Can invite Editors and Guests. Cannot manage billing. |
| Guest | Can View | View-only access. Cannot edit projects or invite others. |

### Model Access Control

The **Model Access Control** setting lets Admins decide how newly released AI models become available to the workspace. You can choose between two defaults:

- **Opt-out (default):** New models are enabled automatically. Members can use them right away, and Admins can disable individual models if needed.
- **Opt-in:** New models are disabled until an Admin explicitly turns them on. Use this mode when you want tighter control over which models your team can access.

Per-model toggles are available either way, so you can mix and match regardless of the default you choose.

## Creating a Workspace

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Workspace Selector">
    Click the workspace selector in the top-left corner of the dashboard.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Create a New Workspace">
    Scroll to the bottom of the dropdown and click **Create a new workspace**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Name Your Workspace">
    Enter a name for the workspace (minimum 3 characters).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a Plan">
    Select a billing plan for the new workspace.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Invite Team Members (Optional)">
    Invite team members by email, or skip this step and invite them later.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Inviting Team Members

Make sure you're in the correct workspace before inviting anyone. You can start an invite from two places:

- The **member count badge** in the top-right of the dashboard
- The **Invite** button on the **People & Seats** page

**Invite by email:** Enter one or more email addresses (separated by commas, semicolons, or spaces), choose a role, and send.

**Invite by link:** Copy a shareable invite link. Choose between an edit link (Editor role) or a view link (Guest role).

## Free Plan Limitations

<Note>
  On the Free plan, each workspace supports a maximum of **3 active projects**. Upgrade to a paid plan to unlock additional active projects.
</Note>

## Workspace Limitations

<Warning>
  Workspaces **cannot be deleted**. If you no longer need a workspace, you can leave it from the workspace settings page.
</Warning>

## Managing Multiple Workspaces

If your team needs to run multiple workspaces under a single bill with a shared usage pool and unified seat management, that's available through **Organizations** — an Enterprise feature. Organizations let you group workspaces together so you pay once and manage everyone from one place.
