The Audio Node closes the loop between what you see and what you hear on the FLORA canvas. Generate a voiceover from a script, produce a sound effect from a plain-language description, score a clip with a text-to-music prompt, or transcribe spoken audio back into text you can wire elsewhere. Audio nodes are fully integrated with the rest of the canvas — you can drive a lipsynced video performance, feed transcriptions into downstream text nodes, and let FAUNA chain audio nodes automatically as part of a multi-step workflow. No separate tools, no booking a recording session.

## Capabilities

The Audio Node supports five distinct workflows. Select a tab to explore each one.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Text to Speech">
    Text to Speech turns a written script into a playable voiceover. Type what you want said, pick a voice, and generate. Use it for narration, scratch VO on concept pitches, character dialogue, and any moment where a voice needs to land on the canvas without a recording session.

    **How to use:**
    - Type or paste your script into the prompt field
    - Open the **voice selector** in the node's floating controls to browse all available voices for the selected model
    - Click the **play button** next to any voice to preview it before committing
    - Switch voices at any time without leaving the node — the voice list updates automatically when you change models
    - Connect the output to a Video Node with a lipsync model to drive a lipsynced performance

    **Expressive delivery:**
    On models that support it, you can use expressive prompt tags (such as *whisper*, *shout*, or *happy*) to shape how the voice delivers the script.

    <Warning>
      **ElevenLabs Multilingual v2 has a 450-character limit per generation.** Text longer than 450 characters is truncated — if your script produces shorter audio than expected, split it across multiple Audio Nodes and chain them together.
    </Warning>

    **Output format:** MP3 at 128 kbps. WAV output is not currently available for ElevenLabs voice models in FLORA.

    **Explore speech models:**
    Visit the [Audio Models](/models/audio-models) section for a full list of available voice models, supported languages, and capabilities.
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Text to SFX">
    Text to SFX generates individual sound effects from a plain-language description. Describe the sound, generate it, and use it as ambient texture, a hit sound, foley, or anywhere you'd otherwise be searching through a stock library.

    **Example prompts:**
    - *"Heavy wooden door slamming shut in a stone hallway"*
    - *"Rain on a tin roof at night, distant thunder"*
    - *"Mechanical keyboard typing rapidly, in a quiet office"*
    - *"Glass shattering on a marble floor"*

    **Best practices:**
    - Be specific about the **source material**, **environment**, and **distance** — more detail produces more convincing results
    - Generate a few variations using different seeds and pick the strongest
    - Layer multiple SFX Audio Nodes together for richer, more textured atmospheres

    <Tip>
      Think of your SFX prompt the way a sound designer thinks about Foley: material + space + action. *"Heavy wooden door"* + *"stone hallway"* + *"slamming"* is more useful than just *"door sound"*.
    </Tip>
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Text to Music">
    Text to Music generates short music tracks from a description of genre, mood, instrumentation, or reference. Use it for background beds on social clips, pitch videos, and mood-setting on ad concepts where you don't want to license a track.

    **How to use:**
    - Describe the track — genre, tempo, instruments, mood, and any stylistic references you have in mind
    - Generate variations across different seeds to find the right feel
    - Pair the music output with a Video Node to produce a fully scored clip on the canvas

    **Example prompts:**
    - *"Cinematic orchestral swell, building tension, strings and brass, no percussion"*
    - *"Lo-fi hip hop, warm vinyl crackle, slow 70 BPM, late night study mood"*
    - *"Upbeat indie pop, acoustic guitar, handclaps, energetic and optimistic"*
    - *"Dark ambient drone, sparse piano notes, unsettling atmosphere"*
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Audio to Text">
    Connect an Audio Node's output to a Text Node and FLORA automatically routes the audio through a speech-to-text model, returning a transcript you can edit, prompt against, and wire into the rest of your workflow.

    **How to connect:**
    1. Add an Audio Node with generated or uploaded audio on your canvas.
    2. Drag a noodle from the Audio Node's output handle to a Text Node's input.
    3. The Text Node automatically switches to Audio to Text mode.
    4. Run the Text Node to receive the transcription.

    **Supported models:** ElevenLabs Scribe, Gemini

    **What to do with the transcript:**
    Once transcribed, connect the Text Node output to another Text Node and prompt it to:
    - Summarize the content in bullet points
    - Pull out key quotes or speaker names
    - Translate into another language
    - Extract any information worth routing downstream

    <Tip>
      Transcription is a powerful bridge between audio and image or video generation. Transcribe a voiceover → extract a scene description → wire it into an Image Node to visualize what the narration describes.
    </Tip>
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Lipsync">
    Pair an Audio Node with a Video Node to drive a lipsynced performance. The Audio Node's output becomes the conditioning signal that synchronizes mouth movement and timing in the generated video.

    **How to connect:**
    1. Generate or upload your audio in an Audio Node (typically speech from Text to Speech).
    2. Connect the Audio Node's output to a Video Node.
    3. Connect a second input to the Video Node — a **Video Node** for lipsync on an existing clip, or an **Image Node** for a talking-avatar generation from a still.
    4. Switch the Video Node's model to **WAN 2.6** or **WAN 2.7**.
    5. Add a prompt to the Video Node to describe the setting and visual style.
    6. Generate.

    <Note>
      The full Audio to Video workflow is documented in detail on the [Video Node](/nodes/video) page, including all available parameters and model options.
    </Note>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Output Format

<Info>
  Audio nodes output **MP3 at 128 kbps**. WAV output is not currently available for ElevenLabs voice models in FLORA. The bitrate is fixed and cannot be changed.
</Info>

## Credit Costs

Audio generation costs vary by model and duration. Before generating, hover over the **Generate** button to see a tooltip showing your estimated credit cost and your available balance. Credits are charged when generation starts. If a generation fails, credits are automatically refunded.

## FAUNA Integration

The Audio Node is **FAUNA-aware** — FLORA's AI agent can create and chain audio nodes for you automatically as part of a multi-step workflow. Ask FAUNA to build a workflow that includes narration, scoring, or transcription and it will place and wire the audio nodes on your behalf.
