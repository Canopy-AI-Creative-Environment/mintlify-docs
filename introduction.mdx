FLORA is an AI-powered creative workflow platform designed for professionals who need more than a single generation — they need a system. On an infinite node-based canvas, you connect over 50 AI models across text, image, video, and audio into structured, repeatable workflows that move at the speed of your ideas. Whether you're a solo designer or a creative team shipping work at scale, FLORA gives you the control and flexibility to ideate, iterate, and refine without limits.

## What FLORA Is

Unlike conventional AI tools that focus on one-off outputs, FLORA is a complete creative operating system. You build workflows — not just assets. By connecting nodes on the canvas, you shape how information and media flow through your creative process, turning individual AI generations into something far more powerful: a reusable, refinable system that grows with your work.

FLORA supports every major creative modality:

- **Text** — large language models for copywriting, scripting, ideation, and structured content
- **Image** — generation, editing, inpainting, upscaling, background removal, and training
- **Video** — generation, animation, lipsync, motion control, and video upscaling
- **Audio** — voice, sound effects, music, and transcription

## Core Concepts

Before you start building, it helps to understand the three foundational concepts in every FLORA project.

**Nodes** are the building blocks of your workflow. Each node represents a single operation — a model, a text input, an image source, a prompt template, or a utility. You configure a node's parameters and run it to produce an output.

**Noodles** are the connections between nodes. When you drag from the output handle of one node to the input of another, you create a noodle — a live data link that passes results (images, text, video, audio) downstream. Noodles are how your workflow flows.

**The Canvas** is the infinite workspace where nodes and noodles live. You can zoom in to work on a single node, zoom out to see the whole system, and pan freely. Nothing is constrained to a grid or a page — the canvas expands as your workflow grows.
