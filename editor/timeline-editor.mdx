The Timeline Editor is FLORA's full video editor, built directly into the canvas as a node. You can assemble multi-track sequences, trim and split clips, add captions and audio, and render a finished MP4 — all without leaving your workflow. Because the Timeline Editor is itself a node, your finished cut flows right back onto the canvas as a new video you can continue building on.

<Note>
The Timeline Editor is a **paid feature** available on Starter plans and above. It is not available on the Free plan.
</Note>

## Getting Started

<Steps>
  <Step title="Add the Timeline Editor node">
    From the node menu, select **Timeline** to add it to your canvas. You can add the node first and connect clips afterward — order doesn't matter.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect source clips">
    Connect clip nodes to the Timeline node. Each connected input becomes a layer, in the order you connected them.

    | You connect… | You get… |
    |---|---|
    | A video node | A video clip |
    | An image node | A still clip (with thumbnail) |
    | An audio node | An audio track |
    | A text node | An editable text layer |
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the editor">
    **Double-click** the Timeline node, or click **Open Timeline Editor** in the node toolbar. The editor opens in place — the node becomes the editing surface while the canvas stays pannable around it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Edit and render">
    Arrange, trim, and style your clips, then open the **Export** panel and choose **Edited File** to render a finished MP4 back onto the canvas.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Multi-Track Timeline

The Timeline Editor supports **up to 10 parallel tracks** of video, audio, text, captions, images, and shapes. Your connected clips start on a single track in connection order — a standard sequential assembly you then refine.

The Timeline Editor **reads clips live from the nodes wired into it** and always uses each node's latest generation. Regenerate a clip while the editor is open and the layer refreshes automatically. Regenerate while it's closed and the layer is up to date the next time you open it.

## Editing Clips

- **Arrange:** Drag clips left or right to reorder or move them. Drag a clip up onto another track to layer it (overlays, text, picture-in-picture).
- **Trim:** Drag a clip's left edge to change its start point, or its right edge to change its end point. Trimming is non-destructive — you're adjusting in/out points, not the source media.
- **Split:** Move the playhead to the exact frame, select the clip, and click the **scissors button** (Split Clip at Playhead). The clip splits into two independent halves.
- **Snapping:** Clips snap to each other and to the playhead. Toggle snapping with `N`.
- **Reorder:** Right-click any clip for the context menu — **Cut**, **Copy**, **Duplicate**, **Bring to front**, **Send to back**, or **Go to Node** (which jumps the canvas view to the clip's source node).

## Captions, Titles, and Text

Add text directly to your video from within the editor. Press `T` to drop a new text item, or connect a text node to bring it in as a linked editable layer.

Style any text item from the properties sidebar: **font** (Google Fonts), **stroke** (outline for legibility over busy footage), **alignment**, plus position, size, opacity, and layer order. Stack text above your video by placing it on a higher track.

**Captions** work as timestamped caption pages — text blocks tied to specific points in time so each line appears at the right moment.

## Audio

Connect an audio node, drag an asset from your library, or upload a file directly into the editor. Each source becomes its own layer so you can run a music bed and a voiceover on separate tracks.

Select any audio clip to adjust **volume**, **playback rate**, **fade in/out**, and **timing**. Video clips carry their own audio too — lower per-clip volume to balance dialogue and music, or use the track **Mute** toggle during playback to check picture on its own.

## Export and Rendering

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Render to MP4">
    Open the **Export** panel and choose **Edited File**. FLORA renders your timeline **server-side** — on FLORA's infrastructure, not in your browser — so you can keep working or close the tab while it runs. The result is an **MP4 (H.264 video, AAC audio)**, saved back onto the canvas as a new version of the Timeline Editor node.

    Rendering is the only metered action in the Timeline Editor — it draws on your workspace **credits**, scaled by the **length and resolution** of the output. The editor shows an estimated cost before you render. Editing and previewing are always free.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Export to a Pro Editor (NLE Bundle)">
    To finish your project in a professional NLE, use the **Timeline + Assets** section in the Export panel. Choose a format and download a ZIP with the project file plus your source media:

    | Format | Opens In |
    |---|---|
    | **EDL** (CMX3600) | Most NLEs |
    | **XML** (FCP7 XML) | Premiere Pro |
    | **FCPXML** | Final Cut Pro, DaVinci Resolve |

    This export is client-side and runs no render — it's always free.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Tip>
**Preview freely, render once.** Lock your cut before exporting — editing and previewing cost nothing, and you're only billed when you render.
</Tip>

## Keyboard Shortcuts

| Action | Shortcut |
|---|---|
| Play / Pause | `Space` |
| Undo | `Cmd/Ctrl + Z` |
| Redo | `Cmd/Ctrl + Shift + Z` or `Cmd/Ctrl + Y` |
| Select all | `Cmd/Ctrl + A` |
| Duplicate | `Cmd/Ctrl + D` |
| Cut / Copy / Paste | `Cmd/Ctrl + X` / `C` / `V` |
| Delete | `Delete` or `Backspace` |
| Toggle snapping | `N` |
| Add text | `T` |
| Add media (import) | `Cmd/Ctrl + I` |
| Export EDL / XML / FCPXML | `7` / `8` / `9` (Export panel open) |

<Warning>
While the Timeline Editor is focused, standard canvas shortcuts are paused — so `Cmd/Ctrl + Z` undoes your last edit in the timeline, not on the canvas.
</Warning>
